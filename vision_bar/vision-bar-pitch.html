05 Reference / Tyndall Effect

Making The Light Path Visible

A fog projection experiment shows how particles can hold light in the air.

The image does not have to land on a flat screen. It can be suspended by mist, liquid, ice, and bubbles.

The projection path becomes part of the work.

06 Imaging Methods

Two Ways To Image Inside A Drink

Wine Glass Reflection A projected image reflects through the curved surface of a wine glass, creating a fragile floating illusion inside the drink.
Refraction, Tyndall, Reflection The cup itself becomes the optical system: liquid refraction, particle scattering, ice, bubbles, and mirrors reveal layers of light.

07 Application / Future

From One Drink To A Temporary Atmosphere

Many existing bars already create beautiful light-and-shadow environments, but the experience often stays around the drink itself.

Vision Bar uses stronger projection to let the drink become the core of a spatial effect.

A glass becomes the anchor for a fleeting atmosphere across the table, the wall, and the surrounding bar space.

A temporary mood, shaped around one drink.
